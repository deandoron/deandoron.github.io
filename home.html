I am a faculty member in the Institute for the Theory of Computing of the Faculty of Computer and Information Science at Ben-Gurion University.

My research lies within Theory of Computation, mainly computational complexity, randomness in computation and explicit combinatorial constructions. Especially, I am interested in pseudorandomness, derandomization, and error-correcting codes.

Before coming to Ben-Gurion, I was a Motwani Postdoctoral Fellow at Stanford and a postdoctoral researcher at UT Austin hosted by Dana Moshkovitz and David Zuckerman. I received my PhD from Tel-Aviv University, where I was fortunate to have Amnon Ta-Shma as my advisor.
